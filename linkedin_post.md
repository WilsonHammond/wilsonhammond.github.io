# Executive Summary

Project Violet Ledger is a simulated Governance, Risk, and Compliance engagement for CivicCare Nexus, a fictional healthcare analytics and payment coordination enterprise.

The scenario examines a third party governance collapse where a vendor contractor credential was exposed through infostealer malware and attackers used dormant privileged Application Programming Interface access for 23 days. The incident created projected exposure of 18.7 million dollars, including legal response, customer notification, incident response consulting, tooling modernization, and cyber insurance implications.

The main conclusion is that the incident was caused by control erosion. CivicCare Nexus had policies, reviews, and monitoring, but the organization lacked durable ownership, evidence discipline, exception governance, and practical human control testing.

## Portfolio Value

This lab demonstrates vendor risk analysis, control gap mapping, human control assessment, cost modeling, executive reporting, and remediation planning. It maps the recovery program against NIST CSF 2.0, NIST SP 800 161, NIST SP 800 53, ISO IEC 27001, and SOC 2 concepts.

## Recommended Recovery Strategy

1. Revoke dormant vendor access and force credential reset
2. Freeze high risk vendor integrations pending review
3. Rebuild supplier inventory and assign named owners
4. Implement access recertification for all third party privileged accounts
5. Establish exception governance with expiration dates and compensating controls
6. Centralize evidence collection in Governance, Risk, and Compliance tooling
7. Report control effectiveness metrics to executive leadership every month
