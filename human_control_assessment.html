I just added a new Governance, Risk, and Compliance portfolio lab: Project Violet Ledger. This one is built around a simulated third party governance collapse at CivicCare Nexus, a fictional healthcare analytics and payment coordination company. The point of the lab was not to treat a vendor incident as a simple technical failure. I wanted to model how risk builds when human controls weaken over time: ownership becomes unclear, reviews become routine, exceptions become permanent, and privileged access survives long after the business reason disappears. The lab includes: • Vendor risk inventory • Risk register • Human control effectiveness assessment • Control gap mapping • Cost exposure model • Remediation roadmap • Executive summary • Portfolio infographic Frameworks used include NIST CSF 2.0, NIST SP 800 161, NIST SP 800 53, ISO IEC 27001, and SOC 2. The strongest lesson from this build is that controls do not fail only because they are absent. Many fail because they exist on paper while behavior, accountability, and evidence quality quietly deteriorate underneath them. That is the GRC problem I wanted to show: not just what went wrong, but how an organization can see the warning signs earlier, assign ownership, reduce residual risk, and restore executive confidence.
