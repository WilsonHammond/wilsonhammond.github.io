# Human Control Effectiveness Assessment

## Why Human Controls Matter

Technology controls can appear healthy while people and process controls quietly decay. In Project Violet Ledger, the most damaging issue was not the absence of policy. It was the gap between policy existence and human execution.

## Key Human Control Failures

### 1. Security Review Fatigue

Analysts accepted vendor evidence because the review process rewarded completion instead of validation. This turned supplier due diligence into a ceremonial control.

Control effect: Supplier review existed, but it no longer proved current vendor security posture.

Metric: Evidence rejection rate after review sampling.

Current state: 8 percent.

Target state: 25 percent.

### 2. Ownership Ambiguity

Procurement believed Security owned vendor reviews. Security believed Legal owned renewals. Legal believed Compliance owned expiration tracking.

Control effect: Vendor lifecycle governance had no durable owner.

Metric: Vendors with named business owner and security owner.

Current state: 54 percent.

Target state: 100 percent.

### 3. Exception Drift

Temporary exceptions remained active without expiration dates, compensating controls, or recurring review.

Control effect: Risk acceptance became a loophole instead of a governed decision.

Metric: Exceptions with expiration dates and compensating controls.

Current state: 41 percent.

Target state: 95 percent.

### 4. Approval Rubber Stamping

Managers approved access requests without verifying business need.

Control effect: Least privilege became dependent on speed and habit instead of judgment.

Metric: Access requests rejected or modified after manager review.

Current state: 3 percent.

Target state: 15 percent.

### 5. Alert Desensitization

Analysts ignored patterns because the monitoring queue was noisy and fatigue became normal.

Control effect: Detective controls lost practical sensitivity even when tools were online.

Metric: False positive rate.

Current state: 67 percent.

Target state: 30 percent.

## Analyst Conclusion

Human controls must be tested like any other control. Completion records are not enough. A mature Governance, Risk, and Compliance program measures decision quality, escalation behavior, approval discipline, evidence skepticism, and control ownership.
