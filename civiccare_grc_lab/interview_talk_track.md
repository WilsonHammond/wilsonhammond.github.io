# Interview Talk Track

## 30 Second Version

I built a fictional Governance, Risk, and Compliance lab around a health technology company that handles healthcare data, consumer financial information, and federal program records. I started by identifying the regulated data types, then mapped the risk areas to Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, Gramm Leach Bliley Act Safeguards Rule, National Institute of Standards and Technology Cybersecurity Framework 2.0, National Institute of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800 53, National Institute of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800 171, and Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program concepts. The deliverables include a risk register, control crosswalk, evidence request list, Plan of Action and Milestones, and executive summary.

## Honest Framing

This was a portfolio lab, not production work. I built it to practice how a Governance, Risk, and Compliance analyst thinks through regulated data, control gaps, evidence, remediation, and residual risk. I am careful not to oversell it as job experience, but I can explain every document and every decision.

## What I Learned

I learned that Governance, Risk, and Compliance is not just matching regulations to controls. The real skill is understanding what data exists, where it flows, who can touch it, what could go wrong, what proof exists, and what risk remains after controls are applied.

## Strong Interview Sentence

The strongest part of the lab is that it treats the cloud issue as a governance problem, not just a technical configuration issue. The risk is not only that a bucket was misconfigured. The deeper risk is that classification, access control, logging, vendor oversight, and incident response were not mature enough to prove regulated data was protected.

## Terms I Can Speak To

1. Inherent risk means the level of risk before additional controls are considered.  
2. Residual risk means the level of risk remaining after controls are applied.  
3. Control objective means what the organization is trying to achieve.  
4. Evidence means the proof that a control exists and works.  
5. Control gap means the difference between what should happen and what can currently be proven.  
6. Risk treatment means the decision to reduce, accept, transfer, or avoid risk.  
7. POA&M means Plan of Action and Milestones, which tracks remediation.  
8. CUI means Controlled Unclassified Information, which requires safeguarding or dissemination controls even though it is not classified.
