# CivicCare Nexus Governance Risk and Compliance Lab

## Portfolio Purpose

This project is a fictional Governance, Risk, and Compliance (GRC) lab built to practice real analyst thinking across healthcare, finance, and federal environments. The goal is not to claim production experience. The goal is to show that I can identify regulated data, map obligations, assess risk, recommend controls, document residual risk, and communicate clearly to technical and nontechnical stakeholders.

## Scenario

CivicCare Nexus is a fictional health technology company that supports 3 business lines.

1. Healthcare services  
CivicCare hosts a patient intake and claims coordination portal for regional clinics. The system stores electronic protected health information, appointment notes, insurance identifiers, claims data, diagnosis references, and provider messages.

2. Patient finance services  
CivicCare also offers payment plans and stores consumer financial information for billing, identity verification, account servicing, and payment processing. The system touches consumer financial information, payment account data, and third party payment vendors.

3. Federal program support  
CivicCare supports a federal health grant reporting pilot. The pilot uses nonpublic grant records, sensitive personally identifiable information, and Controlled Unclassified Information supplied by a federal partner.

## Main Business Problem

CivicCare is preparing for a customer security review after a near miss incident. A misconfigured cloud storage location exposed test records for a short period. The records were synthetic in this lab, but the control weakness would matter in the real world because the production environment handles sensitive health, finance, and federal data.

## Analyst Mission

The analyst must determine whether CivicCare has enough security governance to support regulated data. The work includes data classification, control mapping, risk scoring, evidence requests, corrective actions, and executive reporting.

## Frameworks and Authorities Used

1. Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act Security Rule  
2. Federal Trade Commission Safeguards Rule under the Gramm Leach Bliley Act  
3. National Institute of Standards and Technology Cybersecurity Framework 2.0  
4. National Institute of Standards and Technology Risk Management Framework  
5. National Institute of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800 53 Revision 5  
6. National Institute of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800 171 Revision 3  
7. Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program cloud authorization concepts  
8. Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard 4.0.1 as a payment security reference
