I built a new Governance, Risk, and Compliance portfolio lab that combines healthcare, finance, and federal data risk into 1 practical case study.

The fictional scenario follows a health technology company that supports patient services, patient financing, and a federal health reporting pilot. A cloud storage issue becomes the starting point, but the real focus is bigger than a configuration mistake.

I worked through:

1. Data classification
2. Risk register development
3. Control mapping
4. Evidence requests
5. Vendor risk
6. Incident response decision paths
7. Plan of Action and Milestones documentation
8. Executive reporting

The lab maps concepts from Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act, Gramm Leach Bliley Act Safeguards Rule, National Institute of Standards and Technology Cybersecurity Framework 2.0, National Institute of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800 53, National Institute of Standards and Technology Special Publication 800 171, and Federal Risk and Authorization Management Program cloud authorization language.

I am building this because I do not want a portfolio full of hollow buzzwords. I want to understand the work, speak the language, and show how I think through regulated data, control gaps, evidence, remediation, and residual risk.

Governance, Risk, and Compliance is not just paperwork. At its best, it is how an organization proves it can be trusted.
