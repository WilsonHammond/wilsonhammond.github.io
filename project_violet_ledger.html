Lab 3

Project Violet Ledger

A third party governance collapse and enterprise recovery lab built around CivicCare Nexus, a fictional healthcare analytics and payment coordination company. The lab examines vendor sprawl, stale privileged access, human control erosion, cost exposure, and executive remediation strategy.

$18.7MProjected exposure
23Day detection lag
7Human controls below target

Scenario

A third party analytics vendor retained privileged Application Programming Interface access after a failed Identity and Access Management modernization effort. A contractor credential exposed through infostealer malware allowed unauthorized access to sensitive records. The deeper issue was not only technical compromise. It was control erosion.

Human control failures

  • Security reviews became checkbox routines instead of evidence based evaluations.
  • Temporary vendor exceptions became permanent operating habits.
  • Procurement, Legal, Security, and Compliance did not share clear ownership.
  • Access recertification failed to remove stale vendor privileges.
  • Analysts became desensitized by low quality alerts and delayed escalation.

Framework anchors

NIST CSF 2.0 NIST SP 800 161 NIST SP 800 53 ISO IEC 27001 SOC 2 Third Party Risk Identity and Access Management

Analyst output

  • Vendor risk inventory and service criticality analysis
  • Risk register with inherent risk, residual risk, owners, and treatment strategy
  • Control mapping across Governance, Risk, and Compliance frameworks
  • Human control effectiveness assessment
  • Cost exposure model and remediation roadmap
  • Executive summary written for board level decision making

Key lesson

Controls do not fail only because they are missing. Many fail because they exist on paper while behavior, accountability, and evidence quality quietly deteriorate underneath them. Project Violet Ledger models that slow collapse and shows how a risk analyst can turn it into a measurable recovery plan.
