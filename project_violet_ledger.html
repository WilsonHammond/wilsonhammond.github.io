Lab 4

Project Violet Ledger

A third party governance collapse and enterprise recovery lab built around CivicCare Nexus, a fictional healthcare analytics and payment coordination company. The lab examines vendor sprawl, stale privileged access, human control erosion, cost exposure, and executive remediation strategy.

Lab thesis

This incident is not treated as a single technical failure. It is treated as control erosion. Vendor reviews existed, but review quality declined. Access policies existed, but recertification failed. Alerts existed, but analyst overload reduced detection value. Training existed, but behavior did not change.

Scenario

CivicCare Nexus relies on third party analytics, payment coordination, claims support, and cloud based data processing. A vendor retained privileged access after a modernization project ended. A contractor credential was exposed through malware, and attackers used stale vendor permissions to access sensitive records before detection.

What the lab demonstrates

  • Third party risk management and vendor tiering
  • Human control effectiveness assessment
  • Identity and Access Management drift
  • Security exception governance
  • Control mapping across NIST CSF 2.0, NIST SP 800 161, NIST SP 800 53, ISO IEC 27001, and SOC 2
  • Cost exposure modeling and executive remediation planning

Human controls and effect on controls

The strongest finding is that human behavior changed control performance. Security review fatigue reduced vendor due diligence quality. Executive pressure normalized temporary exceptions. Ownership ambiguity weakened accountability. Approval rubber stamping distorted evidence reliability. Alert desensitization weakened detection. These failures did not remove controls from the environment; they made existing controls less trustworthy.

Remediation focus

  • Rebuild the vendor inventory and assign named owners
  • Revoke stale third party access and recertify privileged accounts
  • Standardize security evidence requests and expiration tracking
  • Create exception governance with expiration dates and executive visibility
  • Measure human control effectiveness instead of assuming training equals behavior change
